# Project 01- Cruisin Texas Webpage

## 1. What Is This?
This repository will serve as a collection for my project which will be a website of listed upcoming Hot Rod Car Shows for the state of Texas and where visitors/attendees can gather more information regarding the venue and the event. The website will act as a resource to find other associated events and to gain more insight to the surrounding areas, accommodations, and amenities.


## 2. Why Does It Exist?
I built this website to give Hot Rod enthusiasts and owners some insight for certain shows they may have considered attending for the first time and unsure of what to expect. My website will give them a better picture of what is offered at these shows so they may be able to plan accordingly when they travel to attend. First time goers who may be traveling will appreciate this, as they can also read reviews from past attendees. The purpose is to provide as much inoframtion as possible to paint a clearer picture of what they can expect when attending the shows, along with what other events may be associated for when after the car show has finished for the day.The site also helps attendees plan beyond the show itself, whether that means booking an overnight stay or exploring local attractions the host city has to offer.


## 3. What Tools Did I Use?
I decided to use Canva to help with the creative workflow and design process. While the platform is very user-friendly overall, I did run into some roadblocks along the way. Certain color shades, fonts, and interactive features I wanted simply weren't available on the free tier, which made things more limiting than I expected.

ChatGPT and Claude were both helpful in pointing me toward alternate tools to fill those gaps, and the workarounds turned out to be solid. For the Shows and Reviews page, I built both submission forms on [JotForm.com](http://JotForm.com). The platform made it easy to customize each form exactly how I wanted by selecting fields, choosing background and field box colors, and setting font styles. Once the forms were finished, I was able to publish them for free without needing to create an account, then exported the embedded links and drop them into my Canva page using the embed app tool.

For the Gallery page, I built two photo viewer grid galleries using widget templates from [Elfsight.com](http://Elfsight.com). The platform was straightforward to work with.  I uploaded and arranged my photos, published the galleries, and embedded the links into Canva the same way. There was a small cost involved, but the photo viewer grid was well worth it. I'm also planning to add more photos from this year's shows as the season goes on.
